## 1. About These Terms

These terms govern your relationship with VARS as a vendor (beauty or grooming professional) operating on the platform. By completing onboarding or accepting a booking, you agree to these terms. If you do not agree, do not use VARS.

"VARS", "we", "us", and "our" refers to the entity operating the VARS platform (CAC-registered, Lagos, Nigeria). "You" and "your" refers to you, the vendor. "Customer" refers to the person who books your services through the platform. "Platform" means the VARS mobile app, website at bookwithvars.com, and any related services.

These terms should be read together with the [Vendor Service Level Agreement](vendor-sla.md), which sets out the operational standards you are expected to meet.

## 2. Your Relationship with VARS

You are an independent contractor. You are not an employee, worker, agent, or partner of VARS. Nothing in these terms creates an employment relationship.

This means:

- You are responsible for your own taxes, tools, products, transport, and professional insurance
- You are free to work on other platforms or independently. There is no exclusivity requirement.
- You control your own schedule, pricing, and service offerings within the platform's rules

VARS provides the technology, payment infrastructure, identity verification, and dispute resolution framework.

## 3. Eligibility and Onboarding

To operate on VARS, you must:

- Be at least 18 years old
- Be based in Lagos, Nigeria (or within the platform's operating area)
- Complete identity verification (KYC) through our third-party provider, Youverify. This includes submitting a government-issued ID and completing a liveness face check (biometric data). You will be asked for explicit consent before biometric processing begins.
- Set up at least one service with a name, category, price, and duration
- Verify a Nigerian bank account via Paystack for receiving payouts
- Grant the app permission to send push notifications (required for receiving booking alerts)

**KYC outcome:**
- Clean pass: you are activated instantly. No admin review required.
- Rejection: you are notified with the reason and can reattempt. Rejected cases are reviewed by VARS admin, who may override or confirm the rejection.

**Profile pre-fill:** If you registered interest on bookwithvars.com before downloading the app, your name, phone number, and service type are pre-filled from your lead record. Pioneer status (if applicable) transfers automatically.

## 4. Your Profile

### 4.1 Services

You define your own services. Each service has a name (up to 60 characters), optional description (up to 200 characters), a category (selected from the VARS taxonomy), a price (minimum NGN 10,000), and a duration (in 30-minute blocks). You may list up to 10 active services.

### 4.2 Profile Photo

Your profile photo is extracted from your KYC liveness check and locked to your account. You cannot change it. This is a trust measure: the photo customers see is the verified identity of the person who will arrive at their door. The platform enforces this at the database level.

### 4.3 Content Rules

Your bio, service names, and service descriptions are filtered to remove phone numbers, email addresses, social media handles, and sequences of 5 or more consecutive digits. This filtering is silent and automatic. Portfolio photos are scanned on your device and rejected if they contain visible phone numbers or social media handles.

These rules exist to protect both you and customers by keeping all communication and transactions within the platform.

## 5. Going Online

You control your visibility to customers with an online/offline toggle. Going online means customers in your area can see your profile and book you. Going offline makes you invisible.

Three conditions must all be met before you can go online:

1. KYC verified
2. At least one active service listed
3. Device notifications enabled

If any condition fails while you are online, you are automatically taken offline. The app checks every 2 minutes and on every screen return.

## 6. Bookings

### 6.1 Receiving Bookings

When a customer books you, you receive a push notification and the booking appears on your jobs dashboard. You have one hour to accept or decline. A reminder is sent at the 30-minute mark.

If you do not respond within one hour, the booking expires. No charge is made to the customer.

### 6.2 Auto-Accept (Optional)

You may configure an auto-accept zone: a geographic area within which bookings are confirmed instantly without manual acceptance. When auto-accept is active:

- You must confirm your zone daily (via the home screen prompt or by saving zone settings)
- Bookings from customers within your zone radius are accepted automatically
- You receive a 5-minute grace period to cancel any auto-accepted booking penalty-free
- After the grace period, standard cancellation rules apply
- If you move more than your zone radius plus 3 km from your zone centre, auto-accept pauses until you return or re-confirm

### 6.3 Status Flow

Once you accept a booking, you must follow these steps in order:

1. **On My Way** - tap when you are heading to the customer. This triggers the customer's payment.
2. **Arrived** - tap when you arrive at the customer's location.
3. **Service Rendered** - tap when you have completed the service.

You cannot skip steps. Each step triggers notifications to the customer, and the payment and settlement process depends on these status transitions happening in sequence.

### 6.4 Access Details and Phone Reveal

The customer's access details (building, floor, flat, gate code) and phone number are revealed to you 15 minutes before the scheduled appointment. The customer receives your phone number at the same time.

You must not store, screenshot, or share customer access details or phone numbers outside the app. These are provided for the specific booking only.

### 6.5 Reschedule

If you cannot make the original time, you may propose a new time. The customer has one hour to accept or decline your suggestion. If the customer declines or does not respond, the booking is cancelled with a full refund.

## 7. Payment

### 7.1 How You Get Paid

VARS operates a subaccount split model via Paystack. When the customer's card is charged (at the point you commit to travel), the payment is split:

- **Your share (80%)** goes directly into your Paystack subaccount
- **VARS platform fee (20%)** is retained in the VARS main account

The split is locked at charge time and cannot change at settlement.

### 7.2 Pioneer Programme

If you are a VARS Pioneer (one of the first 50 vendors to register and verify), you receive 100% of the payment on your first three completed bookings. The platform fee is waived entirely. After three completed bookings, the standard 80/20 split applies. Your Pioneer badge remains on your profile permanently.

### 7.3 Transport Surcharge

When a customer is located more than 5 km from your zone centre, a distance-based transport surcharge is added to the customer's total. The surcharge is included in the split calculation, meaning your 80% share applies to the full amount (service price plus surcharge).

### 7.4 Settlement

Your earnings screen shows three buckets:

- **Cleared**: completed bookings where your share is in your Paystack subaccount
- **Confirming**: bookings marked as service rendered, awaiting customer confirmation or auto-release
- **Under review**: disputed bookings, awaiting admin resolution

"Cleared" means your funds are in your Paystack subaccount. Transfer from your subaccount to your bank account is a separate step triggered by VARS operations. VARS aims to process settlements regularly but does not guarantee a specific transfer schedule at this time.

### 7.5 Settlement Holds

Your entire subaccount balance is held if any of the following are true:

- You have an open or under-review dispute on any booking
- You are in restricted status (see Section 8.2)
- A bank chargeback has been raised against a transaction involving you

The hold applies to all your earnings, not just the disputed booking. This is because Paystack manages holds at the subaccount level.

## 8. Cancellation

### 8.1 Before You Commit to Travel (Pre-Gate)

You can cancel an accepted booking before tapping "On My Way". The customer is not charged (no payment was made), and your cancellation is recorded in your rolling 30-day count.

If you reach 3 or more cancellations in a rolling 30-day period, your account is flagged for review. Flagging may result in temporary suspension.

Auto-accepted bookings have a 5-minute grace window during which you can cancel without it counting toward your cancellation total.

### 8.2 After You Commit to Travel (Post-Gate)

If you cancel after tapping "On My Way" (or after the gate has fired automatically):

1. The customer receives a full refund
2. Your account is immediately restricted (`is_restricted = true`)
3. The refunded amount is recorded as owed by you (`restriction_amount_owed_kobo`)
4. All platform functionality is blocked until the restriction is lifted

To lift the restriction, you must repay the owed amount to VARS through an out-of-band arrangement (not through the app). Once VARS operations confirms receipt, the restriction is lifted via the admin panel.

This policy exists because the customer's card has already been charged based on your commitment to travel. Cancelling after that point costs VARS the refund, which you are responsible for.

## 9. Disputes

When a customer raises a dispute on one of your bookings:

- You are notified immediately
- Your settlement is frozen across all bookings (not just the disputed one) until the dispute is resolved
- A VARS administrator reviews and resolves the dispute, typically within 24 hours
- Possible outcomes: payment released to you, or refund issued to the customer
- You are notified of the resolution

You do not have a direct right to counter-dispute through the app. If you believe a resolution was unfair, contact hello@bookwithvars.com.

## 10. Portfolio Photos and Customer Consent

You may request permission to use a photo from a booking in your portfolio. The process:

1. You submit a consent request through the app
2. The customer is notified and has 72 hours to approve or decline
3. If approved, the photo is published to your portfolio
4. If declined or if 72 hours pass without a response, the photo is not published

You must not use customer photos in your portfolio, social media, or anywhere else without going through this consent flow. Photos obtained outside the platform (e.g. taken on a personal phone) are your responsibility, but VARS strongly recommends using the in-app consent process for any photos involving customers.

## 11. Data Protection

- Store, copy, or share customer personal data (phone numbers, access details, names, locations) outside the VARS app

Customer access details and phone numbers are provided for the specific booking only and must not be retained after the booking is complete. The platform enforces content filtering on your inputs and portfolio uploads to prevent contact-sharing.

For details on how VARS handles your personal data (including your KYC records, earnings, and location data), see our [Privacy Policy](privacy-policy.md).
